Construction projects are often affected by unexpected events that can disrupt the planned sequence of activities, generate delays, and increase project costs. This report presents the development of a rapid-response decision-support tool designed to evaluate the impact of ordinary disruptions on an existing construction work plan. Starting from an Excel-based schedule organized on an hourly basis, the tool allows the user to simulate different risk events, such as adverse weather conditions, lack of workers, or machinery failures, and to identify both directly and indirectly impacted activities. The framework evaluates delay propagation through predecessor–successor relationships and compares alternative recovery strategies aimed at reducing or eliminating the delay. Recovery actions are based on the increase of operational teams and, when necessary, additional machinery, while considering the related extra costs. The tool also integrates cost data derived from the Regione Lombardia official price list, allowing the economic impact of corrective actions to be assessed. The final application includes a graphical user interface and automatically generates Excel outputs containing updated work plans, impacted activities, recovery details, scenario comparisons, and cost summaries. Although some simplifications were introduced, such as the linear relationship between workforce increase and activity duration, the proposed framework represents a practical and intuitive starting point for supporting project managers in the rapid assessment of construction-site disruptions and the selection of cost-effective recovery strategies.
Keywords: Schedule; Delay analysis; Risk evaluation; Decision-support tool; Recovery strategies.
